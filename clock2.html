History:

While the exact time of the clock's origin is uncertain, one of the earliest moments of landmark achievement in the tool's history came with John Harrison's marine chronometer.
The chronometer, an 18th century innovation for those navigating the seas, took Harrison more than 30 years to perfect.
The chronometer was one of the earliest recorded instances of the use of a precise, accurate, and portable timepiece with the ability to determine longitude by measuring the time of a fixed location, such as Greenwich Mean Time, and the time at the user's current location.
